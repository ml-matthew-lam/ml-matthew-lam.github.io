Deblurring U-Net + Ablation Study

In this project, I designed a nonlinear-activation-free U-Net architecture ("Mini-NAFNet") for motion deblurring. I then trained the network, achieving an average 27.2 PSNR and 0.832 SSIM on the GoPro_Large test set. I also tested the effect of replacing the SimpleGate operations with GELU over shorter training durations. The Mini-NAFNet outperformed the architecture with GELU activations (+0.75 PSNR, +0.023 SSIM), consistent with literature.

Background

The goal of a deblurring model is to take in a blurred image as input, and output a sharp version. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs), such as U-Net architectures, are often used for deblurring.

Traditional "vanilla" CNNs use nonlinear activation functions like ReLU or GELU. Many image restoration models also use channel attention, which involves the sigmoid function. However, image restoration U-Net architectures that removed or replaced these activation functions have been found to work well (even better than conventional architectures). One such architecture is the NAFNet (nonlinear-activation-free network) introduced by Chen et al., and my model, which I'll refer to as a mini-NAFNet, is based on that one.

In addition to designing and training my mini-NAFNet, I also wanted to experiment with replacing a key element from the NAFNet architecture to see for myself how this would impact performance. NAFNet uses SimpleGate in place of nonlinear activation functions. Instead of passing a tensor \(x\) through an activation function, SimpleGate slices \(x\) into two chunks \(x_1\) and \(x_2\), then multiplies the chunks elementwise: $$ \mathrm{SimpleGate} (\mathbf x) = x_1 \odot x_2. $$ In my experiment, I tested the effect of putting nonlinear activations back into my mini-NAFNet, i.e., putting GELU back in the place of SimpleGate, to obtain what I'll call model-G, for "GELU." This is essentially the same ablation carried out by Esaulov and Esfahani, which found that the NAFNet outperformed the model with GELU.
