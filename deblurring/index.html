Deblurring U-Net + Ablation Study

In this project, I designed a nonlinear-activation-free U-Net architecture ("Mini-NAFNet") for motion deblurring. I then trained the network, achieving an average 27.2 PSNR and 0.832 SSIM on the GoPro_Large test set. I also tested the effect of replacing the SimpleGate operations with GELU over shorter training durations. The Mini-NAFNet outperformed the architecture with GELU activations (+0.75 PSNR, +0.023 SSIM), consistent with literature.

Background

The goal of a deblurring model is to take in a blurred image as input, and output a sharp version. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs), such as U-Net architectures, are often used for deblurring.

Traditional "vanilla" CNNs use nonlinear activation functions like ReLU or GELU. Many image restoration models also use channel attention, which involves the sigmoid function. However, image restoration U-Net architectures that removed or replaced these activation functions have been found to work well (even better than conventional architectures). One such architecture is the NAFNet (nonlinear-activation-free network) introduced by Chen et al., and my model, which I'll refer to as a mini-NAFNet, is based on that one.

In addition to designing and training my mini-NAFNet, I also wanted to experiment with replacing a key element from the NAFNet architecture to see for myself how this would impact performance. NAFNet uses SimpleGate in place of nonlinear activation functions. Instead of passing a tensor \(x\) through an activation function, SimpleGate slices \(x\) into two chunks \(x_1\) and \(x_2\), then multiplies the chunks elementwise: $$ \mathrm{SimpleGate} (x) = x_1 \odot x_2. $$ In my experiment, I tested the effect of putting nonlinear activations back into my mini-NAFNet, i.e., putting GELU back in the place of SimpleGate, to obtain what I'll call model-G, for "GELU." This is essentially the same ablation carried out by Esaulov and Esfahani, which found that the NAFNet outperformed the model with GELU.

Architecture Design

Put the updated diagram here, plus design choices.

  • The architecture was made relatively lightweight in terms of the channel depth, the number of levels, and the number of blocks per level. This is so that training would not take too long, and so if a bug were to be found during training, it would not cause too much of a delay.
  • A global residual connection was added connecting the input directly to the output so that the U-Net would only have to learn the difference between the blurred and sharp images, not the entire images themselves.
  • Bilinear upsampling was used instead of up-convolutions to avoid checkerboard artifacts.

Training

For training, I used a combination of \(\mathcal L_1\) loss and SSIM loss. SSIM is defined as $$ \mathrm{SSIM} = \frac {(2\mu_x \mu_y+c_1)(2\sigma_{xy}+c_2)} {(\mu_x^2+\mu_y^2+c_1)(\sigma_x^2+\sigma_y^2+c_2).} $$ where \(\mu_x, \mu_y, \sigma_x^2, \sigma_y^2, \sigma_{xy},\) are the averages, variances and covariance for patches of images \(x\) and \(y\).

Specifically, I used the loss function $$ \mathcal L = \alpha \mathcal L_1 + (1-\alpha) \mathcal L_\mathrm{SSIM} $$ where \(L_\mathrm{SSIM}=1-\mathrm{SSIM}\) and \(\alpha = 0.16\) as suggested by Zhao et al..

Results

I tested the trained models on the test split of GoPro_Large, and measured average SSIM and PSNR of the outputs. PSNR is calculated as \(\mathrm{PSNR} = -10 \log_{10}(\mathrm{MSE})\), since the expected range for the values in the image tensors is \([0,1]\).

Takeaways + Discussion

My mini-NAFNet was quite effective at deblurring images, given its relatively small size.

For image restoation, NAFNet-like models can be more effective than architectures that replace SimpleGate with GELU.
